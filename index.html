Milestone Status Report

Since we’re using HW4 as a launch point for our project, we first forked one of our HW4 repositories and began to modify it for our project. Firstly, the existing cloth is 2D, whereas we need a 3D object to represent our block of mercury or butter. We immediately noticed the exponential growth in the number of springs required to turn the 2D cloth into a 3D block. We went to office hours and discussed this with a TA, and we decided to represent our 3D block as point masses connected by structural springs only. Theoretically, we would not need springs at all to represent our solid, since we can just have point masses set at fixed distances from each other. However, since the shading program relies on the presence of springs, we decided to connect the point masses with the bare minimum amount of springs. Since the springs are only there for the shader to work, we also modified the simulation rendering by turning off the gravity and setting the spring constant to 0, effectively nullifying the effects of the springs and giving us a solid block of point masses.

During OH, we also discussed with the TA how we should go about implementing our heat transfer interactions. Since heat transfer is very complicated with many components, we simplified our model by making some assumptions. We assume an absolute zero surrounding environment (so we would only need to consider heat transfer from a point heat source), simple conduction between point masses, and potentially interpolating some temperature dependent properties like viscosity from material databases we find online.

We attempted to render the 3D solid using the adjustments described above, but we are currently stuck on a segfault issue with the shader application. For debugging, we first made sure our point masses are being rendered in the correct positions, and that the proper spring connections are being made. Furthermore, we ensured that we didn’t have stray spring connections leading to nowhere. We narrowed the issue down to the shader, which is what we are working on currently. We suspect the problem may be related to the shader being designed for a 2D implementation.

Reflecting on our project progress, we realize that we need to first roll back some of the things we wanted to do. Trying to program a realistic 2D/3D heat transfer solver and visualizer is going to take quite a while and may be computationally heavy given our devices. Thus, we are not going to try to recreate our simulation in another market CFD software. To make our lives easier, we are also going to make more assumptions and decisions about what we are trying to render to simplify the coding problem.

In terms of our work plan, we are mainly cutting out this step of recreating our simulation in another software like Ansys or Abaqus. We are still aiming for some visual accuracy in our simulation while having the simulation run in reasonable time. Phase change is something we also need to dive deeper into, but we are still planning to render the melting of a block of mercury or butter into some kind of liquid representation.
